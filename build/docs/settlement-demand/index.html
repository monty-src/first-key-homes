Settlement Offer

caution

This letter constitutes FORMAL FINAL NOTICE before we initiate legal proceedings. Your company's failure to maintain the property at 3017 Colvard Parkway, Charlotte, NC 28269 in a fit and habitable condition, as required by N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-42 (Landlord Duties), constitutes a material breach of our lease agreement and the Implied Warranty of Habitability under North Carolina law.

Your pattern of neglect over the past five months—specifically regarding the continued failure of the HVAC system and resulting secondary issues—has caused substantial damage, including but not limited to:

I. Preliminary Warning and Legal Context​

  • Property Damage and Diminished Use.
  • Severe disruption to the quiet enjoyment of the premises.
  • Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress.
  • Potential Child Endangerment due to uninhabitable living temperatures/conditions.

We have exhausted all good-faith attempts to resolve these critical issues. Unless a settlement is reached by the deadline below, we will immediately file a lawsuit seeking all remedies available under North Carolina law, including treble damages.

II. Settlement Offer - Primary Demand​

To settle all current and potential claims arising from your breaches, we demand the following comprehensive settlement package.

ComponentDemand SpecificationRationale/Authority
Monetary Damages$35,000.00 (Lump-Sum Payment)Compensating for five months of diminished value, emotional distress, time spent managing repairs, and statutory damages.
Lease TerminationImmediate, mutual termination without penalty or fee.Ending the tenancy due to material breach by the Landlord.
Security DepositFull return of the deposit ($3,232.00) plus accrued interest.Required by N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-52.
Property RepairsImmediate scheduling and commencement of full HVAC replacement (not repair) within 7 calendar days; completion within 14 calendar days.Addresses the core breach of habitability.
Reporting/ReferenceWritten positive rental reference and a mutual non-negative reporting covenant (no reporting to credit bureaus or tenant screening agencies).Essential to prevent punitive financial/housing damage.
Outstanding Credits$477.16 to be included in the lump-sum payment or credited immediately.Acknowledged outstanding balance.

III. Alternative Settlement Options (Mutually Exclusive)​

caution

Should the Primary Demand not be acceptable, we are willing to consider the following alternative options to facilitate an immediate resolution:

A. Immediate Departure and Maximum Compensation​

  1. $40,000.00 Cash (Lump-sum payment).
  2. Lease terminated immediately.
  3. Full Security Deposit returned within 48 hours.
  4. Moving Expense Reimbursement of $2,000.00.
  5. 30 days to peacefully vacate the premises following receipt of payment.
  6. All other non-monetary terms of the Primary Demand (Positive Reference, No Negative Reporting) must be met.

B. Rent Reduction and Stay​

  1. $28,000.00 Cash (Lump-sum payment).
  2. 50% Rent Reduction for January 2026, February 2026, and March 2026.
  3. Full HVAC Replacement commenced within 7 days.
  4. Right to terminate the lease at any time before the reduced period ends, without penalty, upon 7 days' written notice.

IV. Acceptance Deadline and Terms​

  • Acceptance Deadline: December 17, 2025, at 8:00 PM EST.
  • Payment Due: Within seven (7) calendar days of signing a mutual settlement agreement.
  • Deal Breakers: No payment plans will be accepted. Settlement requires a lump-sum payment. We will not agree to any non-disclosure provision that prevents us from posting factual reviews regarding the conditions and your company's conduct.

V. Consequences of Non-Settlement​

If a signed settlement agreement is not executed by the Acceptance Deadline, our offer is automatically revoked, and we will proceed immediately with full legal and regulatory action without further notice.

1. Litigation​

  • A lawsuit will be filed in the appropriate North Carolina court seeking:
    • Treble Damages for breach of N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-42, currently estimated at $147,210.00.
    • Punitive Damages for willful and reckless conduct, estimated between $10,000.00 and $50,000.00.
    • Statutory Attorneys' Fees and Court Costs.

2. Regulatory and Public Action​

  • Filing of formal complaints with the NC Real Estate Commission (NCREC) and the Better Business Bureau.
  • Filing a complaint with the Fire Marshal/Mecklenburg County Code Enforcement regarding the electrical issues/HVAC hazards.
  • Public documentation of the uninhabitable conditions via factual and non-defamatory online reviews and appropriate media outlets.

Please be advised: We have retained all communication records, photographic evidence, and repair service notes. We urge you to immediately forward this demand to your corporate counsel and liability insurance carrier.

We look forward to your timely acceptance.
