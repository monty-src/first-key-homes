Court Filing Preparation

Small Claims vs. Superior Court​

Small Claims: Under $10,000

  • Faster
  • Simpler process
  • No attorney required
  • Limited to actual damages

Superior Court: $10,000+

  • Better for treble damages claim ($147,210)
  • Full discovery
  • Jury trial available
  • Attorney recommended

Recommendation: Superior Court (treble damages claim)

Complaint Structure​

Include Counts:

  1. Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability
  2. Breach of Landlord Maintenance Duty
  3. Breach of Contract
  4. Unfair and Deceptive Trade Practices (Treble Damages)
  5. Negligence
  6. Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress
  7. Breach of Covenant of Quiet Enjoyment
  8. Constructive Eviction

Evidence Exhibits​

Required:

  • ☐ Lease agreement
  • ☐ Water bills (5 months)
  • ☐ Temperature logs
  • ☐ A&W Heating report
  • ☐ Communication records (selection)
  • ☐ Photos (organized)

Supporting:

  • ☐ Financial records
  • ☐ Payment history
  • ☐ Code enforcement report
  • ☐ Vendor estimates

Witness Preparation​

Plaintiff Testimony:

  • Overview of tenancy
  • Issues and timeline
  • Impact on family
  • Professional damage
  • Emotional distress

Witnesses to Call:

  • A&W Heating technician
  • Code Enforcement inspector
  • Family/friends (emergency help)

Timeline for Court​

Filing to Trial:

  • File complaint: Dec 16
  • Defendant served: Dec 20
  • Initial hearing: Jan 10-20
  • Discovery: Jan-Feb
  • Mediation: Mar-Apr
  • Trial: May-Jun

Expect 5-6 months from filing to trial

Settlement During Litigation​

Most cases settle during:

  • After discovery (parties see evidence)
  • During mediation (judge helps negotiate)
  • Before trial (uncertainty factors)

If goes to trial: Likely wins with high damages due to:

  • Clear liability
  • Jury sympathy
  • Complete documentation
  • Child endangerment component
