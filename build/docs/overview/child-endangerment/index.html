Here is the rewritten section for Issue #2: Child Endangerment:

This issue stems directly from the failure in Issue #1, but FirstKey's response to the known presence of a 3-year-old child in an uninhabitable home constitutes a separate and severe act of willful negligence and child endangerment.

Exposure and Risk Assessment​

FirstKey Homes was explicitly notified that a 3-year-old child was exposed to dangerous indoor temperatures for over 70 hours.

The Landlord's Duty to Protect​

NC law grants extra protection to tenants under the age of 18. FirstKey had an enhanced, non-delegable duty to ensure the home maintained a safe and healthy temperature for the child.

FirstKey's Knowing Endangerment​

The most egregious failure is FirstKey's deliberate choice to categorize this situation as merely "Urgent" rather than an "Emergency", directly contradicting the facts provided by the tenants.

1. Electrical Fire Hazard Ignored​

The parents made the decision to stay overnight based on the immediate promises of support from representatives Narvi and Yhen. The family was then forced to choose between cold exposure and a fire hazard:

  • Explicit Warning: Narvi and Yhen were clearly informed that the broken portable heaters, if plugged in, tripped the electrical breaker and presented a potential electrical fire hazard, which could cause more harm than good.
  • The Decision to Freeze: The family endured the freezing cold rather than risk a fire, demonstrating the severity of the temporary "solution" FirstKey provided.

2. Failure to Provide Safe Alternative​

Despite multiple opportunities, FirstKey failed its duty to provide a safe alternative for the child, directly leading to the family's separation.

  • Refusal of Hotel: The promised hotel accommodation was never delivered, and later, representative Rachel actively refused accommodation, instructing the family to seek assistance through their Renter's Insurance.
  • Forced Removal: The parents were ultimately forced to remove the 3-year-old from the home due to the sustained, dangerous temperatures and FirstKey's complete failure to act.

Impact and Consequences​

Physical Dangers​

  • Risk of Illness: Exposure to 57°F for days significantly increased the child's risk of respiratory illness.
  • Sleep Disruption: The child could not sleep comfortably, leading to physical and emotional stress.
  • Forced Removal: The child was displaced from her home and separated from her parents during a critical time (the holiday season) due to the landlord's negligence.

Emotional and Psychological Impact​

  • Trauma: The 3-year-old child was subjected to prolonged psychological stress, repeatedly asking, "Why is our house so cold?"
  • Displacement: Family separation and the destruction of holiday traditions cause significant emotional injury to a child of this age.

Conclusion: FirstKey Homes' decision to label a 57°F house with a tripping electrical breaker (fire risk) and a child inside as "urgent" instead of "emergency," and subsequently breaking promises for accommodation, demonstrates a complete and reckless disregard for the child's life and safety. This is a primary factor supporting a claim for Treble Damages under the Unfair and Deceptive Practices Act.

Takeaways​

FirstKey Homes' response to this heating failure demonstrates a reckless disregard for the safety of a minor residing in the property.

  • Willful Endangerment: FirstKey was explicitly informed of the 3-year-old child's presence, the life-threatening internal temperature 57°F, and the fire hazard posed by their temporary solution (tripping electrical breakers).
  • Failed Duty of Care: The repeated classification of the situation as merely "Urgent" instead of an "Emergency" by the Charlotte Local Office is a demonstrable failure to meet the required legal standard of care for a minor.
  • Breach of Promise: The broken promise of immediate hotel accommodation, coupled with the instruction to rely on Renter's Insurance, confirms that FirstKey prioritized cost avoidance over the immediate health and safety of the child.
  • Forced Separation: FirstKey's negligence directly resulted in the forced removal and displacement of the 3-year-old child from her home, causing unnecessary trauma and disrupting family life during the critical holiday season.
