# Legal Violations

## Overview

FirstKey Homes has violated multiple North Carolina statutes protecting tenant rights and creating landlord duties to maintain habitable premises.

## Primary Statutes Violated

### N.C.G.S. § 42-42: Implied Warranty of Habitability

**The Statute:**
> "A landlord shall provide fit premises and shall make all repairs necessary to keep the premises fit and habitable."

**FirstKey's Violations:**
- Failed to maintain functional plumbing (5+ months)
- Failed to maintain functional heating (51+ hours at 57°F)
- Failed to maintain electrical system for safe temporary heating
- Property not fit for occupancy with 3-year-old at 57°F

**Tenant Remedies Available:**
- Withhold rent (via escrow)
- Terminate lease without penalty
- Sue for damages
- Seek rent abatement

### N.C.G.S. § 42-38: Landlord's Duty to Maintain

**The Statute:**
> "A landlord shall keep and maintain in good working order and promptly repair all electrical, plumbing, heating, ventilating, air conditioning and other equipment."

**FirstKey's Violations:**
- Did not keep heating in good working order (failed system)
- Did NOT promptly repair (51+ hours without heat)
- Did not keep plumbing working (leak for 5+ months)
- Did not maintain electrical for safe operation

**Standard: "Prompt" means immediately, especially for emergencies**

### N.C.G.S. § 42-44: Tenant Remedies

**What Law Provides:**
If landlord fails to maintain habitability, tenant may:
1. Terminate rental agreement without penalty
2. Withhold rent (via escrow)
3. Make repairs and deduct from rent
4. Sue for damages

**Tenants Have Done:**
- ✅ Provided written notice (multiple times)
- ✅ Landlord failed to remedy in reasonable time
- ✅ Established rent escrow
- ✅ Exercising right to pursue legal action
- ✅ Reserving right to terminate lease

### N.C.G.S. § 75-1.1: Unfair and Deceptive Trade Practices

**The Statute:**
> "Unfair methods of competition in or affecting commerce and unfair or deceptive acts or practices in or affecting commerce are declared unlawful."

**FirstKey's Violations:**

1. **False Promises - Hotel Accommodation**
   - Promised but never provided
   - Clear misrepresentation
   - Made to stop complaints

2. **False Promises - Callbacks**
   - Multiple promises to call
   - None were fulfilled
   - Pattern of deception

3. **False Promises - Repair Credits**
   - Promised $1,161.41 credit
   - Only paid $684.25
   - $477.16 still unpaid
   - No explanation for shortfall

4. **Deceptive Delay Tactics**
   - "Approval process" used indefinitely
   - No timeline ever provided
   - Deliberately obscure explanations
   - Used to avoid taking action

5. **Knowing Misrepresentation**
   - Offered broken portable heaters as "solution"
   - Knew vendor said replacement needed
   - Misrepresented adequacy of temporary solution

**Remedy: Treble (3x) Damages Available**

The law allows 3x damages for willful unfair or deceptive conduct. FirstKey's conduct qualifies:
- Deliberate pattern of broken promises
- Bad faith customer service
- Vendor-confirmed repairs ignored
- Knowing child endangerment
- Systematic delay tactics

## Additional Legal Claims

### Breach of Contract

**The Lease Contains:**
- Premises maintained in habitable condition
- All utilities in working order
- Landlord responsible for repairs
- Tenant right to safe, warm home

**FirstKey Breached:**
- Material term (habitability) violated
- Multiple breaches documented
- Tenant damages directly caused by breach

### Breach of Covenant of Quiet Enjoyment

**The Doctrine:**
Every lease has implied covenant that tenant can enjoy home peacefully and undisturbed.

**FirstKey's Breach:**
- Heating emergency destroyed peaceful enjoyment
- Constant contractor visits/disruptions
- Multiple phone calls needed for follow-up
- Cannot use home for intended purpose (live in)
- Child cannot play/live normally

### Negligence

**Elements:**
1. Duty: Maintain safe, habitable premises ✅
2. Breach: Failed to maintain heating/plumbing ✅
3. Causation: Failures directly caused damages ✅
4. Damages: Financial, professional, emotional ✅

**FirstKey clearly liable for negligence**

### Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress (NIED)

**Elements:**
1. Extreme/outrageous conduct ✅ (knowing child endangerment)
2. Severe emotional distress ✅ (documented for 3 months)
3. Causal connection ✅ (direct result of FirstKey's conduct)
4. Severity ✅ ($15,000+ claim for emotional distress)

**FirstKey's conduct qualifies:**
- Negligent disregard for child safety
- Deliberate delay while child in danger
- Pattern of broken promises creating psychological impact
- Emotional distress clearly foreseeable and severe

### Constructive Eviction

**The Doctrine:**
If landlord's actions make premises uninhabitable, tenant constructively evicted even if not physically removed.

**Application Here:**
- 57°F home = uninhabitable
- Child had to be actually removed for safety
- Tenants deprived of home use
- FirstKey caused this condition
- Tenant right to terminate lease without penalty

### Child Endangerment

**Possible Claims:**
- Knowingly allowed child in dangerous condition (57°F)
- Refused safe alternative (hotel)
- Offered inadequate temporary solution (broken heaters)
- Delayed emergency repair
- Created unsafe electrical/fire hazard

## NC Building & Housing Codes

### International Property Maintenance Code

**Section 602.3 - Heating Systems:**
- Must be capable of maintaining 68°F during winter heating season
- 57°F = clear violation
- Landlord must ensure compliance

**Section 505.1 - Plumbing Systems:**
- Must be maintained safely and properly
- Related electrical systems must not create hazards
- Broken heaters + electrical issues = violation

**Section 605.1 - Electrical Systems:**
- Must be maintained in safe condition
- Appliances must operate safely
- Breaker constantly tripping = safety violation

### Charlotte-Mecklenburg Local Codes

**Charlotte Housing Code:**
- Minimum heating capability: 68°F
- Landlord maintenance responsibility
- Emergency repairs = immediate
- Code enforcement authority to inspect and cite

## NC Landlord-Tenant Law

**Key Principles:**

1. **Habitability is non-waivable**: Tenant cannot waive right to habitable premises
2. **Prompt repairs required**: Immediate for emergencies
3. **Good faith required**: Landlord must act in good faith to resolve
4. **Tenant remedies**: Withhold rent, terminate, sue, repair-and-deduct
5. **Bad faith multipliers**: Unfair practices allow treble damages

**FirstKey violates all principles**
