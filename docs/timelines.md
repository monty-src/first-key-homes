## **Heating System Outage & Repair Timeline**

```mermaid
timeline
  title Heating System Outage & Repair Timeline

  December 9 : 12:36 PM – Initial loss of primary heat reported (Work Order #2611471)
             : Property management stated replacement parts unavailable
             : Primary heating system non-functional
             : Maintenance delivers 2 heaters only 1 works.
             : Child temporarily removed from the home due to cold conditions

  December 10 : Vendor A&W Cooling inspected HVAC
              : Determined system discontinued in 2001
              : Vendor conclusion: full HVAC replacement required
              : Portable heaters provided as temporary measure
              : Use of heaters caused electrical breaker trips and power loss
              : No reliable heat available overnight

  December 11 : Maintenance restored electricity
              : Portable heaters continued to be incompatible with electrical system
              : Primary heating system still non-functional
              : No reliable heat available

  December 12 : Second vendor (Benchmark) inspected HVAC
              : Confirmed full replacement required
              : Temporary fireplace use suggested
              : Primary heating system still non-functional
              : No reliable heat available

  December 13 : Primary heating system still non-functional
              : No reliable heat available

  December 14 : Primary heating system still non-functional
              : No reliable heat available

  December 15 : Primary heating system still non-functional
              : No reliable heat available

  December 16 : Maintenance revisited electrical system
              : Confirmed original portable heaters were too powerful for circuits
              : New lower-voltage heaters provided
              : Instructions given to limit usage (one heater per room; outlet restrictions)
              : Primary heating system still non-functional

  December 17 : Permanent HVAC replacement scheduled to begin
              : Code Enforcement time 9:00 AM
              : Vendor start time 9:30 AM
              : Vendor end time 1:30 PM 
              : Primary heating system still now functional
```

- **December 9**
  - Heat stopped working.
  - Maintenance indicated parts were unavailable and would need to be ordered.
  - Home became uninhabitable due to cold; **child was temporarily removed from the home**.
- **December 10**
  - Vendor **A&W Cooling** confirmed the unit was **discontinued in 2001**, requiring full replacement.
  - Temporary heaters were provided.
  - Temporary heaters caused **electrical issues**, including breaker trips.
  - Emergency maintenance requests were made; **no heat overnight**.
- **December 11**
  - Maintenance restored electricity, but **heat remained unavailable**.
- **December 12**
  - Second vendor (**Benchmark**) confirmed **full HVAC replacement required**.
  - No permanent heat restored.
- **December 13–15**
  - Home continued to have **no reliable heat**.
- **December 16**
  - Maintenance acknowledged the previously provided heaters were **too powerful for the electrical system**.
  - New heaters with lower voltage were provided.
  - Maintenance instructed:
    - Only **one heater per room**
    - **Do not use certain outlets**
- **December 17**
  - **Code Enforcement** inspection at 9:00 AM.
  - **Permanent HVAC Replacement** starts at 9:30 AM.
  - :star: Heat restored by 1:30 PM.
